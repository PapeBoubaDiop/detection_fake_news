About the Application

This web application was developed as part of a consulting initiative for the Federal Communications Commission (FCC), the independent U.S. agency that regulates both the content and infrastructure of communications at the federal level.

The goal of this application is to provide a tool capable of automatically identifying fake news articles, particularly those announcing disasters or other sensitive topics. Since it is not always easy to determine whether an article is real or fake, the FCC wishes to use machine learning to assist in this classification task.

The model used here is a Multinomial Naive Bayes classifier trained on a labeled dataset of news articles. It is part of a pipeline that includes preprocessing techniques such as text normalization, frequency filtering, and TF-IDF vectorization.

Important: This application is designed to process and analyze articles written in English only. Submitting texts in other languages may lead to incorrect or unreliable predictions.

Prediction results are visually indicated as follows:

  • Green = Likely true information
  • Red = Likely fake news

This project has educational value and can also serve as a basis for future developments. It can be improved with multilingual support, more extensive datasets, or deep learning-based classifiers.
